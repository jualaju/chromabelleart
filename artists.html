Meet the Artists

Discover the visionary creators behind the masterpieces and learn about their lives, techniques, and artistic journeys

Vincent van Gogh

1853 - 1890

Dutch

A post-impressionist painter who created about 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, most of them in the last decade of his life.

Post-Impressionism
Famous Works:
  • The Starry Night (1889)
  • Sunflowers (1888)
  • Self-Portrait (1889)

Salvador Dalí

1904 - 1989

Spanish

A surrealist artist known for his striking and bizarre images. His artistic repertoire included painting, graphic arts, film, sculpture, design and photography.

Surrealism
Famous Works:
  • The Persistence of Memory (1931)
  • The Elephants (1948)
  • Metamorphosis of Narcissus (1937)

Johannes Vermeer

1632 - 1675

Dutch

A Dutch Baroque Period painter who specialized in domestic interior scenes of middle-class life. He was a moderately successful provincial genre painter.

Dutch Golden Age
Famous Works:
  • Girl with a Pearl Earring (1665)
  • The Milkmaid (1658)
  • View of Delft (1661)

Katsushika Hokusai

1760 - 1849

Japanese

A Japanese artist, ukiyo-e painter and printmaker of the Edo period. He is best known for his woodblock print series Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji.

Ukiyo-e
Famous Works:
  • The Great Wave off Kanagawa (1831)
  • Fine Wind, Clear Morning (1831)
  • Thunderstorm Beneath the Summit (1831)

Grant Wood

1891 - 1942

American

An American painter and representative of Regionalism, best known for his paintings depicting the rural American Midwest. He is particularly known for American Gothic.

Regionalism
Famous Works:
  • American Gothic (1930)
  • Daughters of Revolution (1932)
  • Stone City, Iowa (1930)
