The Great Wave off Kanagawa

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai

Year: c. 1829-1833

Location: Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York

The Great Wave off Kanagawa is a woodblock print by the Japanese ukiyo-e artist Hokusai. It was published sometime between 1829 and 1833 in the late Edo period as the first print in Hokusai's series Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji.

The image depicts an enormous wave threatening boats off the coast of the town of Kanagawa. The wave is set against the background of Mount Fuji. It is Hokusai's most famous work and is often considered the most recognizable work of Japanese art in the world.
