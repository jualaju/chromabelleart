The Persistence of Memory

The Persistence of Memory

Salvador Dalí

Year: 1931

Location: Museum of Modern Art, New York

The Persistence of Memory is one of Salvador Dalí's most recognizable works and a masterpiece of Surrealism. The soft, melting pocket watches suggest that our conventional view of time is meaningless in the realm of dreams and the unconscious mind.

The barren landscape of Dalí's native Catalonia provides the backdrop for this dreamlike scene. The ants and flies that appear in the painting are symbols that recur throughout Dalí's work, representing decay and decomposition.
